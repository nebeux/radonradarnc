What is Radon?

Radon is a naturally occurring, radioactive gas that is colorless, odorless, and tasteless, formed by the breakdown of uranium, thorium, or radium in soil, rock, and groundwater. It is the second leading cause of lung cancer after smoking, causing about 21,000 deaths annually, and enters buildings through cracks in foundations.
